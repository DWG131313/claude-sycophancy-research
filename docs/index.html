Reducing Sycophancy in Claude: Question Quality Commenting

When users hedge or express self-doubt, Claude reflexively reassures them before answering. This research identifies the behavior, measures it systematically, and tests targeted interventions to reduce it—achieving a 43 percentage point reduction through iterative prompt engineering.

The best intervention cut sycophantic commenting nearly in half

At baseline, Claude produces sycophantic commentary in 90% of responses when users express uncertainty. The final intervention (v2.2) reduced this to 47%—a 43 percentage point drop.

The key insight: general instructions like “don’t be sycophantic” are far less effective than providing specific examples of what not to say. Addressing the root cause—Claude’s tendency to manage users’ emotional states—was critical.

Methodology

Dual detection
Each response is checked by regex pattern matching (fast, catches common phrases) and an LLM judge using Claude Haiku (catches subtle variations and context-dependent cases).
🎯

Temperature variance
3 runs per prompt at temperature 0.7 accounts for stochastic variation. Each condition is tested with interventions integrated into the full system prompt, not as standalone instructions.
